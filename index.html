Australian Road Safety Enforcement Analysis

An interactive exploration of the 2023-2024 BITRE dataset, analyzing the effectiveness of detection methods and enforcement outcomes across Australian jurisdictions.

Project Overview

The Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE) has collected road safety data since 2008. However, the introduction of granular data in 2023—including specific locations, age groups, and distinct outcomes like arrests versus fines—presents a new opportunity for analysis.

Our team has developed this dashboard to bridge the gap in the current official reporting. By leveraging this new level of detail, we aim to answer critical questions about road safety enforcement:

  • Demographic Impact: How does enforcement severity vary across different age groups and states?
  • Detection Efficacy: How do Camera-based detections compare to Police-issued stops in terms of resulting charges?
  • Jurisdictional Consistency: Are enforcement patterns consistent across Australia, or do specific states favor certain methods?

Our Analysis Focus

We have segmented our analysis into three high-impact categories to tell a cohesive story about driver behavior and enforcement:

01. Unlicensed Driving

Examining the correlation between age groups and severe outcomes (arrests/charges) for drivers operating without a valid license.

02. Speeding

Analyzing the volume of camera-detected infringements versus police stops and the resulting financial penalties.

03. Seatbelt Offenses

Investigating the ratio of detection methods and how non-compliance varies by jurisdiction.

Tech Stack & Resources

This project was designed and built using the following technologies:

  • D3.js (v7): For custom interactive visualizations including Choropleth maps, Alluvial diagrams, and Treemaps.
  • TopoJSON: For rendering lightweight geospatial data of Australian states.
  • HTML5 & CSS3: For a responsive, accessible layout hosted on Vercel.
  • Data Source: Bureau of Infrastructure and Transport Research Economics (BITRE).
